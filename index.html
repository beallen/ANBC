Facts About Bees

Facts About Bees
  • Pollinators are a crucial component of terrestrial ecosystems because of the pollination services they provide.
  • Currently, they are under threat from a number of factors including, habitat loss, pesticide use, novel diseases, and climate change.
  • Our understanding of pollinators throughout Alberta is lacking.
  • The federal government lists 321 wild bee species as occurring in Alberta.


Monitoring Bees in Alberta

Monitoring Bees in Alberta
  • In 2018, the Alberta Native Bee Council organized a group of volunteers across Alberta to sample Alberta's native bee populations.
  • Since our survey, there has been a proliferation of publicly available data on native bees in Alberta.
  • We compiled data from organiziations including: Alberta Conservation Association, Stickland Entomological Museum, Alberta Environment and Protected Areas, and the Global Biodiversity Information Facility.
  • Explore the Bee Atlas to see where species were detected, their specific habitat associations, and which areas of Alberta are rich in bee biodiversity.
